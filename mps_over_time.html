Women in Parliament

Celebrating 100 Years of Women in the House of Commons

This year marks a century since women in the United Kingdom were first given the right to vote in and stand for elections. Today, in early 2018, there are 208 women from various parties and politicial persuassions serving their constituents in the House of Commons. So how did we arrive here? And what contribution have women MPs made to democracy in the UK? Let's dive in and take a look!

Countess Markievicz: first woman MP

1918 → 1922
Dublin, St Patrick's
In December 1918, Countess Markievicz became the first woman elected to the House of Commons. However, she was a member of Sinn Féin and due to a party policy of abstentionism, she did not actually take her seat. A socialite-turned-revolutionary, she spent her life campaigning on behalf of working people, fought in the Easter Rising against the British, and got herself elected to the first Irish Dáil (parliament) while serving a jail sentence.
